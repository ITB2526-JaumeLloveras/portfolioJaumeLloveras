CREATION OF NEW HIGHWAY ACCESSES

RESEARCH PROJECT | 2ND BACCALAUREATE 2024-2025

Climate Change Impact on Maresme Road Infrastructure

A comprehensive research project analyzing the imminent threat of sea-level rise on coastal roads (N-II National Road) and railway infrastructure in Maresme due to climate change. The study proposes innovative solutions for maintaining regional mobility through new highway infrastructure.

üéØ Project Overview

This research project investigates the consequences of climate change on Maresme's coastal infrastructure, specifically focusing on the potential disappearance of the N-II National Road due to rising sea levels.

Key Objectives:

  • Analyze current traffic patterns across 30 municipalities
  • Simulate impact of N-II disappearance
  • Design new highway infrastructure (J-17)
  • Create interactive GIS mapping solution

üî¨ Methodology

The research was conducted in three main phases:

Phase 1: Real-time traffic monitoring using Catalan Traffic Map
Phase 2: Hypothetical impact simulation dividing towns into 3 categories
Phase 3: Solution design using Google Maps layered methodology

üìä Key Results

Hypothesis 1: CONFIRMED

N-II disappearance would cause catastrophic mobility collapse

Hypothesis 2: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED

Solutions exist but require massive investment and long-term planning

üí° Proposed Solution

J-17 Highway System:

A new inland highway running parallel to C-32, distributing traffic across 7 strategic groups of municipalities.

The solution includes:

  • 20+ new highway access points
  • Local connector roads between towns
  • Improved C-32 entrances
  • Complete traffic redistribution system
